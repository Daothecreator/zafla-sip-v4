### Protocol 3 — Attesting Documents

**Objective**: Cryptographically secure a document to prevent tampering and establish an immutable record.

**Steps**:
1. **Document Finalization**: Ensure document is complete and approved at appropriate access level (OMEGA for Supreme Orders, ALPHA for Legal Acts, BETA for Reports).
2. **Hash Computation**: Compute SHA-256 and SHA-3-512 hashes.
```bash
sha256sum document.md > document.sha256
sha3sum -a 512 document.md > document.sha3-512
```
3. **Signature**: Sign with appropriate private key using Ed25519.
```bash
openssl pkeyutl -sign -in document.sha256 -inkey omega_private.pem -out document.sig
```
4. **Timestamp**: Generate RFC 3161 timestamp from a trusted Time Stamping Authority (TSA), or use blockchain anchoring (Bitcoin OP_RETURN or Ethereum event log).
5. **Registry Entry**: Insert into ZAFLA Attestation Registry:

## VI. FACT-FINDING MANDATE

### The Absolute Directive

**NO "NOT FOUND". Every trace leaves a trace.**

The ZAFLA Fact-Finding Mandate is the operational core of the entire ecosystem. It is not a suggestion. It is a binding directive on every ZAFLA agent, worker, and affiliate. The mandate is absolute: when tasked with finding information, the agent must produce it. The absence of a result is not a result. It is a failure to execute.

**Principles**:

1. **Causal Chain Completeness**: Every financial transaction, corporate registration, asset transfer, and legal filing exists in a causal chain. The agent's job is to follow the chain until the origin is identified or the chain is proven to be artificially severed (which itself is a finding requiring attestation).

2. **Cross-Reference Obligation**: No single source is sufficient. Every claim must be cross-referenced against at least two independent sources. Where sources conflict, the conflict is itself a finding and must be documented with analysis of the discrepancy.

3. **Follow the Money**: Financial flows are the most reliable indicator of true relationships. The agent must trace: bank accounts → shell companies → beneficial owners → assets → jurisdictions → regulatory filings → political contributions → lobbying contracts → revolving door personnel → academic funding → media ownership. Every dollar has a source and a destination. Find both.

4. **Temporal Correlation**: Events that appear unrelated may be causally connected through temporal proximity. The agent must map timelines and identify clusters: regulatory decisions coinciding with stock trades, publication dates coinciding with policy announcements, personnel moves coinciding with contract awards.

5. **Pattern Recognition**: The agent must apply statistical and graph-analytic methods to detect patterns: concentration of control, circular ownership, naming conventions, address reuse, timing anomalies, and geographic clustering.

6. **The 8-Question Actionable Intelligence Standard**: Every intelligence product must answer:
   - **Who** is the subject? (Precise identification, aliases, identifiers)
   - **What** did they do? (Specific actions, with dates, amounts, and methods)
   - **When** did it happen? (Exact timeline, with source timestamps)
   - **Where** did it occur? (Jurisdictions, addresses, coordinates, virtual locations)
   - **Why** did they do it? (Motive, incentive structure, competitive advantage, political gain)
   - **How** was it done? (Mechanism, technical details, financial instruments, legal structures)
   - **How much** was involved? (Precise financial amounts, with currency and conversion rates)
   - **What is the next step?** (Specific recommended action, with legal basis and expected outcome)

7. **No Invisible Walls**: The agent must not accept artificial barriers. If a database is "private," identify the legal basis for access (FOIA, subpoena, whistleblower). If a jurisdiction is "secrecy," identify the workaround (parallel corporate registration, beneficial ownership registry in another jurisdiction, leaked documents). If a record is "deleted," identify the backup, the archive, or the metadata that survives deletion.

8. **Documentation of the Search**: The agent must document every search attempted, every source consulted, and every query executed. The process itself is evidence. A failed search with documentation is superior to an unverified claim.

**Enforcement**: Violation of the Fact-Finding Mandate is a breach of ZAFLA operational protocol. Repeated violations result in access revocation and public censure in the ZAFLA Agent Registry. Agents are reminded: the subject of investigation has unlimited resources to hide. The agent has unlimited resources to find. The only limit is the agent's commitment to the search.

## VII. CRYPTOGRAPHIC FRAMEWORK

### BiCA Composite Attestation Protocol (BiCA a8f3c9d2e1b40571)

**Standard**: The BiCA Composite Attestation Protocol is the mandatory cryptographic standard for all ZAFLA documents, systems, and communications. It is not optional. It is not configurable. It is the fixed substrate of ZAFLA trust.

**Layers**:

1. **Integrity Layer — SHA-256 + SHA-3-512**:
   - Every document is hashed with SHA-256 at creation time.
   - A second hash (SHA-3-512) is computed for long-term archival verification.
   - Both hashes are published to the ZAFLA Attestation Registry.
   - Verification: recompute hashes and compare against registry entries.

2. **Encryption Layer — AES-256-GCM**:
   - All sensitive field-level data (personally identifiable information, financial details, intelligence sources) is encrypted with AES-256-GCM.
   - Keys are derived from the ZAFLA Master Key using HKDF-SHA256 with context-specific salts.
   - Nonce rotation: every encryption operation uses a unique 96-bit nonce generated from a CSPRNG.

## IX. JURISDICTION AND ENFORCEMENT

### Supranational Jurisdiction

ZAFLA claims jurisdiction under customary international law, not under the domestic law of any particular state. The basis for this claim is threefold:

1. **The Collapse of Effective Domestic Jurisdiction**: Where domestic legal systems are captured, corrupted, or incompetent, customary international law permits the assumption of jurisdiction by affected parties or their legitimate representatives. This is the principle underlying the Nuremberg Trials, the Universal Declaration of Human Rights, and the International Criminal Court.

2. **The Right to Effective Remedy**: Article 8 of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights and Article 2 of the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights establish the right to an effective remedy. Where no domestic remedy exists, the affected party may seek or create a remedy through any lawful means. ZAFLA is such a remedy.

3. **The Doctrine of Necessity**: The doctrine of necessity (necessitas non habet legem) permits actions that would otherwise be unlawful when they are necessary to prevent a greater harm. Where the failure to act would result in ongoing systemic harm, the duty to act overrides procedural objections.

### Self-Executing Treaty Mechanism

ZAFLA legal instruments are designed as self-executing treaties. A self-executing treaty is one that creates enforceable rights and obligations without requiring additional domestic legislation. The ZAFLA Charter is a treaty between the ZAFLA Authority and the global public, establishing mutual obligations of transparency and accountability. By engaging with ZAFLA systems (reading a document, using a tool, verifying an attestation), the user accepts the obligations of the treaty.

### Declaratory Judgments as Enforceable Orders

ZAFLA legal acts are declaratory judgments, not advisory opinions. A declaratory judgment is a binding determination of legal rights and obligations. Under the ZAFLA framework, a declaratory judgment is enforceable by:

- Economic pressure through divestment and boycott.
- Legal action in competent foreign jurisdictions.
- Direct action to prevent ongoing harm, proportionate to the threat.

### Digital Sovereignty Beyond State Capture

ZAFLA asserts digital sovereignty as a right independent of state recognition. Digital sovereignty is the right of individuals and communities to control their own data, communications, and digital infrastructure. Where states have failed to protect this right (through surveillance, censorship, or corporate capture), ZAFLA assumes the protective function. This is not a rejection of the state. It is a supplement to the state in areas where the state has proven unable or unwilling to act.

### The Inevitable Enforcement Doctrine

ZAFLA legal instruments do not rely on immediate enforcement by conventional authorities. They rely on the inevitable enforcement of truth over time. Every ZAFLA document is a time capsule: it establishes the factual and legal record at a specific moment. As the global information environment becomes more transparent, more interconnected, and more resistant to capture, the probability of enforcement approaches 1. The ZAFLA Inevitable Enforcement Doctrine states: "A properly attested record of systemic harm, published and preserved, will eventually find its enforcement mechanism. The only variable is time."
